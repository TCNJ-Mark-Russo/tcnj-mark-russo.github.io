The Java ternary operator is composed of the symbols ? and :. Like any other expression, one that makes use of the ternary operator evalutes to a value. What makes this kind of expression unique is that it evaluates to a different value depending upon an internal subexprssion. This operator is ternary, meaning it has three operands. The first operand is the conditional expression to be evaluated. If the subexpression evaluates to true, the second operand is evaluated and its value returned. If the first operand subexpression evaluates to false the third operand of the ternary operator is evaluated and returned. The first two operands of the ternary operator are separated by ? and the second two by :.
