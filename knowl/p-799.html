You are familiar with how DoodlePad’s graphic objects can handle events such as a mouse click or key press. In Subsection 4.6.1 we learned how to associate a method with an event by passing a method reference to a special method such as .setMousePressedHandler(…). DoodlePad provides a second way to handle events. When extending a shape or the Pad class, you may override certain inherited methods as a way to handle associated events rather than assigning a method reference. This approach can be more convenient and intuitive to implement.
