As your programs grow in size, you will define more classes, many of which may end up with identical method and field implementations, especially when they are related in some way. This ends up making program maintenance very difficult and violates the DRY principle. This is a tedious and error-prone process. Sooner or later a mistake will be made. It is much better to define the shared methods once, and let each of the class make use of the single implementation. This is one majr benefit of inheritance.
